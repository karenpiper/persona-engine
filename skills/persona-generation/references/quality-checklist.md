# Persona Quality Checklist

Run this checklist before finalizing any persona. A persona that fails multiple checks should be revised before output.

## Completeness

- [ ] All required fields are populated (marked * in template)
- [ ] Demographics are specific enough to be useful, not so specific they're arbitrary
- [ ] At least one behavioral observation goes beyond the obvious
- [ ] Pain points describe *tensions*, not just inconveniences
- [ ] The "relationship to category" section is specific to this brand/product space, not generic

## Strategic Utility

- [ ] A creative team could use this persona to make a tonal decision without asking questions
- [ ] The "what resonates / what doesn't" section gives real direction, not vague adjectives
- [ ] The persona could be used to evaluate a campaign idea ("would Maya respond to this?")
- [ ] If there are multiple personas, each one represents a genuinely distinct segment

## Authenticity

- [ ] The direct voice sample sounds like a real person, not a brand writing about a person
- [ ] No attribute reads as a stereotype without evidence
- [ ] At least one insight is surprising, specific, or counter-intuitive
- [ ] The persona has internal coherence — their values, behaviors, and pain points hang together

## Differentiation (multi-persona sets)

- [ ] Each persona has a distinct archetype label that clearly differs from the others
- [ ] The personas don't overlap so heavily they could be merged into one
- [ ] The contrast between personas is made explicit somewhere in the output
- [ ] No persona is simply a demographic average or "ideal customer"

## Red Flags to Fix

**Too vague**: "Values quality and convenience" — every persona does. Get specific.
**Too neat**: Personas with no tensions or contradictions aren't real people.
**Demographic-only**: If the persona is basically a census profile with some adjectives, it needs more psychographic depth.
**Brand-centric**: Personas should describe how the person sees themselves, not just how they relate to your product.
**Invented specificity**: Don't invent a persona's name, city, or job title to make it feel real unless there's a reason. Archetypal truth matters more than false precision.
