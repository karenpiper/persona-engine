# Persona Courtroom — Question Sets

Reference file for `/persona-courtroom`. Three tiers designed to test different layers of the work. Select by tier, mix across tiers, or add custom questions on top of any set.

---

## How to Choose

| Tier | Use When | Tests |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Tier 1 — Strategy** | The strategic brief or platform is what's being evaluated — before creative enters the room | Whether the foundation is sound: insight truth, audience understanding, category knowledge, competitive differentiation, ownability |
| **Tier 2 — Concept** | A creative idea is being evaluated — independent of how it's executed | The idea itself: clarity, emotional resonance, strength, extensibility, tagline, call to action |
| **Tier 3 — Execution** | Specific executions are being evaluated — how well they bring the idea to life | Execution quality: first impressions, clarity, relevance, visual/craft, impact, distribution fit, brand impression |

Tiers can be combined. Running Tier 1 + Tier 2 together is common when a brief and early concept are being tested in the same session.

---

## Tier 1 — Strategy Evaluation

*Used to assess whether the strategic foundation is sound — before creative enters the room.*

### The Problem
1. What challenge or tension does this brand, product, or category face in your mind?
2. What would need to be true for you to think differently about this brand?

### The Insight
3. Does this feel like it understands something real about you or people like you?
4. When you encountered this, did anything feel surprisingly true? *(If yes: What? Why?)*

### Audience Understanding
5. Does this feel made for you, or for someone else? *(If someone else: Who? Why?)*
6. Does this feel like it knows who you are — your life, your priorities, your mindset?

### Category / Culture / Company Knowledge
7. Does this feel informed about the world you live in?
8. Does it reflect how you actually think about this category?

### Competition
9. Does this feel different from what you've seen or heard from other brands in this space? *(If so: How? What does everyone else say or do?)*
10. Is there a brand you feel does this better? *(If so: Why?)*

### Brand Relevance & Ownability
11. Does this feel like it could only come from this brand — or could any brand say this?
12. Does this give you a reason to believe? *(What makes it credible — or not?)*

## Tier 2 — Creative Concept Evaluation

*Used to assess the idea itself — independent of how it's executed.*

### Concept Clarity
1. What's the central idea here? Can you say it in your own words?
2. Was the overall idea clear, or did anything feel confusing?

### Emotional Resonance
3. How does this make you feel? *(Probe: Why that?)*
4. What is the primary emotion you walk away with?

### Idea Strength
5. Does this feel like a big idea or a small one? *(Why?)*
6. Does this feel fresh and original, or familiar? *(If familiar: Where have you seen something like this?)*

### Idea Legs / Extensibility
7. Can you imagine this idea living in different places — TV, social, out of home, events? *(Where?)*
8. Is this something you'd want to share with others? *(Who? How?)*

### Tagline / Campaign Line
9. What do you think of the campaign line?
10. What does it mean to you?
11. Does it feel like the right words for this idea? *(What words would you use instead?)*

### Motivation / Call to Action
12. What does this idea make you want to do, think, or feel differently about? *(Why?)*

---

## Tier 3 — Creative Execution Evaluation

*Used to assess how well specific executions bring the idea to life.*

### First Impressions
1. Would you say your overall reaction is mostly POSITIVE or mostly NEGATIVE?
2. What words come to mind immediately after seeing this?

### What Worked / What Didn't
3. Is there anything you saw, heard, or read that you liked?
4. Is there anything you saw, heard, or read that you disliked?

### Clarity
5. What is this execution communicating to you?
6. Was the message clear?

### Relevance
7. Is this meaningful to you, or do you see it as more for someone else? *(Why? Who?)*

### Visual Presentation / Art Direction
8. What do you notice about the visual presentation?
9. What do you think it's trying to convey? *(Why do you say that?)*

### Impact
10. What does this motivate you to do, if anything?

### Distribution / Context
11. Where would you expect to see or hear this?

### Cross-Execution Comparison *(use when multiple executions are being evaluated)*
12. Which execution resonates with you most? *(Why?)*
13. Which visual treatment best brings this idea to life? *(Why?)*

### Overall Preference *(use after all concepts and executions are shown)*
14. Which concept did you connect with most overall? *(Why?)*
15. Do any of these feel like they're speaking directly to you — personally and authentically?

### Brand Impact
16. What impression do these ideas give you about the brand?
17. Does this change how you think or feel about the brand? *(How? Why?)*
18. Does this feel like a brand you trust or want to engage with? *(Why?)*
19. Would you seek out this brand now that you've seen this? *(What would you do next?)*

## IBM-Specific Add-On

*Bolt onto any tier when the input is IBM-specific (watsonx, IBM Security, IBM Consulting, Red Hat, etc.)*

1. Does this change how you think about IBM — or does it confirm what you already thought?
2. What part of this feels like the "old IBM" versus the IBM IBM wants to be?
3. If this were from a competitor (AWS, Microsoft, Google), would it land differently? Why?
4. What's the one IBM-specific claim here that you'd need to validate before accepting?
5. Does this give you what you'd need to have a productive conversation with IBM? What's still missing?

## Custom Question Guidance

When users want to add their own questions, suggest these as starting prompts:

- **The belief test**: What's the claim in this input that requires the most trust — and what does that reveal about the audience relationship?
- **The peer test**: Would someone in this persona's network share this, forward it, or quote it?
- **The competitive test**: Where does this most directly compete with how a key competitor positions?
- **The timing test**: Does this land differently at different points in the buying journey? Where is it strongest?
- **The fear test**: What's the thing this input is implicitly telling the audience not to be afraid of? Is it the right fear?
- **The proof test**: What evidence would make the central claim airtight — and is that evidence present?

---

## Courtroom Configuration Notes

- **Maximum recommended questions per round**: 7. Beyond that, persona responses become unfocused.
- **Recommended configurations**:
  - Strategy brief only: Tier 1, questions 1–12 (full set) or a focused subset of 5–7
  - Concept only: Tier 2, questions 1–12 (full set) or focused subset
  - Execution only: Tier 3, select by what's being tested (impressions, clarity, brand impact)
  - Brief + concept together: Tier 1 (5 questions) + Tier 2 (5 questions) = 10 total, across 2 focused areas
  - Full evaluation: Run Tier 1 in Round 1, Tier 2 in Round 2 — use Round 3 for cross-tier synthesis
- Users can mix across tiers: "Questions 3–4 from Tier 1 + questions 5–8 from Tier 2"
- Users can add 1–3 custom questions on top of any preset configuration
